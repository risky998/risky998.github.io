My Favorite Projects

Cornell Secret Santa 2019

At Cornell, academic, professional and social pressures can suddenly become overwhelming and exhausting.

We wanted to create a Christmas event that would bring the community together to destress before finals and allow students to meet new people and make new friends through a campus-wide Secret Santa. We created a React web app and a matching algorithm in Python to do this. Check out Cornell Secret Santa!



Ezrabox

Students at Cornell often cannot take all of their items back with them over long academics breaks such as summer break.

We created Ezrabox to connect students at Cornell with potential hosts (also at Cornell) who have extra space in their apartments, allowing students to store their items over the summer at an affordable price. I worked mainly on the backend, working with Flask and PostgreSQL to create a functional application. Check out Ezrabox!



Bioinformatics Kit

In Fall 2019, I took a fascinating class on Computational Genomics at Cornell. Based on the material taught in this class, I have worked on developing algorithms that could be used to solve some "classic" problems in the field - finding the optimal seqeuence aligment for two strands of DNA and identifying CG islands in DNA strands.

I am also working on building a Hidden Markov Model to accurately identify gene-coding regions of Prokaryotic DNA when given a DNA sequence. Part of the model is live at my GitHub.
