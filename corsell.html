Project: Corsell

A second-hand buy sell platform @ Cornell University

Corsell - An Overview

The idea for Corsell originated at a Hackathon in 2018, when my group decided we wanted to address the inflated prices of textbooks purchased from both online stores as well as from the physical store at Cornell. We soon realized that this second-hand marketplace for goods did not have to be limited to textbooks and could be expanded to other household appliances, lifestyle goods and even the subletting of houses. The only source of second hand goods at the time were a number of Facebook "buy-sell" groups, which were messy and disorganized. We wanted to centralize this entire process.

My Work on Corsell

I personally worked on helping create a functioning backend in Flask, capable of connecting with a PostgreSQL database. We are currently under the process of improving our application and are undergoing a complete redevelopment process, using Firebase (A NoSQL Database), Redux-Sagas and ReactJS to create a functional application. We are also trying to create a mobile version of the product.
